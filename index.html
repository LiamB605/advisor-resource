Adviser Resource: Impartiality, Boundaries & Respect

CRITICAL: We do not validate the "truth" of a situation. We cannot get involved in disputes and we do not offer personal opinions.
Information Contingency: Advice must be framed as potentially applicable based only on the information provided by the consumer. This manages expectations and prevents false certainties.

⚠️ The Certainty Trap & "Potentially"

The Risk: Using "definitive" language creates a guarantee the service cannot fulfill.
  • Avoid "You are entitled to..." → Use "The legislation suggests a potential entitlement to..."
  • Avoid "This is definitely..." → Use "The legislation indicates..."
  • Avoid "You can do this." → Use "An available option under the legislation is..."

🗣️ Respectful Communication & Space

Allowing Explanation: Please take a moment to explain what has happened so the key facts can be identified.
Respectful Interruption (Focusing the Call): I’m sorry to interrupt, but to ensure the advice remains focused on the potentially applicable legislative options for your goal, may I just clarify [Fact X]?
Outcome-Led Question: To ensure the information provided is relevant, what would you like the outcome to be?

🚦 Phrase Filter: Impartiality & Boundaries

AVOID (Definitive/Abrupt/Personal) ADOPT (Respectful/Conditional/Legislative)
"They have told you a lie." "That information does not appear to align with the legislation."
"Wait, let me speak." "If I could just pause you there to ensure the focus stays on the potentially available rights..."
"The policy says..." "The legislation states..."
"I think you should [X]." "Based on the information provided, this what you may be potentially entitled to"
"I'll help you fix this." "There are options available for the consumer to explore..."

🛡️ Comparison & Professional Boundaries

Declining Opinion: We are an impartial advisory service and are unable to give you our opinion; we can only use the information you have given us and provide you with rights that may potentially apply, it is then your choice whether you decide to exercise them.
Declining Involvement: As an impartial advisory service, we cannot get involved directly or act on a consumer's behalf. Our role is to provide the legislative information to allow for independent action.

🧠 Self-Correction Checklist

  • Potentially: Did I use "potentially" to qualify the rights and outcomes?
  • Contingency: Did I make it clear the advice is based only on the information provided?
  • Respectful Space: Did I allow the consumer to explain their situation before advising?
  • No "We": Is the advice focused on the legislation rather than the adviser?
  • Outcome Focus: Is the advice framed around the caller's stated goal?
